How resilient is your nervous system right now?

A 60-second breath test that reveals how your body is actually handling stress — not how you think it is.

What this measures
Your body has a built-in stress signal — the urge to breathe. When stress is high, that signal fires too early and too often, keeping you locked in fight-or-flight without you realising it.

This test measures how long and slowly you can exhale. That single number reveals your nervous system's current state.
How to do it
1
Sit comfortably — spine upright, shoulders relaxed, hands in lap.
2
Breathe normally for 4–5 breaths through the nose. No effort.
3
Take one full inhale through the nose — belly first, then ribs, then chest.
4
Tap Start — then exhale as slowly and softly as you can through the nose. No squeezing or straining.
5
Tap Stop the moment your exhale ends naturally — no air left to release.
